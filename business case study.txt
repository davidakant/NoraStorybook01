How would I deliver the final product to them for use on an iOS or Android tablet?
For this use case (one-off personalized content per order, delivered electronically, used on iOS and Android), I'd recommend: host it at a private link and have them "Add to Home Screen." Two tempting alternatives are worse fits:

A downloadable zip they open locally seems appealing ("they own a file forever") but iOS Safari doesn't handle local file:// HTML with linked JS/CSS/images reliably — there's no clean "double-click index.html" experience on a tablet. This would be a support headache specifically on the platform you most care about.
A real native app via the App Store/Play Store is overkill and basically unworkable for a made-to-order business — each customer's storybook is unique content, and app stores aren't built for "submit a new app per order" (cost, review delays, and Apple's policies against single-purpose/template apps all work against you).
So, the practical path:

Host each order at its own URL (an unguessable slug, e.g. yoursite.com/nora-x7k2, not something indexed or guessable — these are photos of someone's kid). Netlify, Vercel, Cloudflare Pages, or GitHub Pages all have free tiers that comfortably cover this at low volume.
Deliver via Etsy as a PDF/message with the link and a QR code plus one-line instructions: "Open this in Safari/Chrome, tap Share → Add to Home Screen." This app already has the right meta tags for that (apple-mobile-web-app-capable, etc.) — it'll launch full-screen with its own icon, no browser chrome.
Add a small service worker to cache the app's assets on first load, so after that it works fully offline like an installed app — no recurring internet dependency, no ongoing cost difference from step 1, just one more file.
